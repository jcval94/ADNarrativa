These are movie scenes that led to lawsuits. So in The Hangover Part II, after Stu got a facial tattoo similar to Mike Tyson's, the movie studio was sued by Mike Tyson's tattoo artist for copyright infringement on the design. They ended up settling for an undisclosed amount. In a scene from a Family Guy episode from 2006, Carol Burnett sued the show for $2 million for copyright infringement and claimed that the scene tarnished her image, but the judge dismissed the case because it was protected under parody law under the First Amendment. In Back to the Future Part II, the director had an actor wear prosthetics in a scene to look like the original actor who played George McFly, who didn't return for the sequel. However, the original actor ended up suing the studio for unauthorized use of his likeness, and he actually won. In Borat, during this scene when Borat was taking an etiquette lesson, he was sued by the etiquette coach for fraud and deception as she was operating under false pretenses, but the judge ended up dismissing it because she signed a creative release form prior to shooting. And finally, in the 1997 movie The Devil's Advocate with Keanu Reeves, the studio was sued for using a copyright-protected sculpture in this scene and they ended up having to edit it out of 475,000 VHS tapes.
